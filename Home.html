Why Mpumalanga?

Mpumalanga is the most scenic province of this nation. Each season, tourists visit this place for various reasons and destinations. The gallery below will give you a glimpse of some of the beautiful attractions Mpumalanga is blessed with.

Blyde Canyon

This is the Blyde Canyon of the Mpumalanga Province. It is located near a town called Graskop. It is one of the most visited destinations in Mpumalanga. The views from the top are breathtaking and the valleys below look amazing.

There is vast land around the area, many hiking places, and swimming pools for relaxing experiences.

Mpumalanga's rainforests are home to an incredible variety of plants, birds, and small animals.

God's Finger

God's Finger is a striking natural rock formation along the Panorama Route. It resembles a giant finger pointing skyward and offers dramatic scenic views.

Exploring the forests around the Panorama Route can feel like stepping into another world.

Echo Caves

The Echo Caves near Ohrigstad are among South Africa's oldest cave systems. They are famous for their stalactites, stalagmites, and underground chambers.

These forests are not only natural wonders but also hold cultural significance for local communities.
